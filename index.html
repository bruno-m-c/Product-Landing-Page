Tiger

Tiger, lithe and strong, even
As age has crept up on
Him, fur dense with mats,
His own personal dreadlocks,
My joke hiding grief. He is
Sweet, all his sharper edges
Work away, leaving behind
A beautiful cat, a creature I want
To hang onto forever.
Holding him in your arms, up close,
On the left side as he likes it,
You have that sensation of
Touching and knowing
A remarkable breakable thing, a miracle.

Lily

Lily, hen-like, a mother
Settling herself onto her
Nest, rests close to my face
After his customary turn under the
Covers, climb back out onto
My pillow, as close to my
Features as he can get it,
My mouth and nose often
Obscured by his love, his
Warmth with me on the coldest
Of days. He sleeps, and so do I.
When he wakes he is affectionate
And whining, that loud sound of
His heard all over the room,
Such trust when he
Leaps to my lap, cold nose in the
Crook of my elbow, head
Slowly sinking as he falls, again, asleep.

Cassie

Cassie, he of the sweetest face-kisses
You could imagine, one side, other,
Kiss kiss, as Latino a cat as you could
Wish. His eyes the softest foam
Green, fur lush as rugs and velvet,
His heart made of gold. He climbs
Atop us in bed, kneads himself
To happiness, flops down
With contented sigh,
His weight warm and heavy,
Limbs numbing if I am
Not careful. He is a constant
When I make or serve food,
His piercing cries of hunger
Nearly convincing me he must
Be on the brink of death,
Until I relent, set down an
Empty dish for him to so thoroughly
Clean.
